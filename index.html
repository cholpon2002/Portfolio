Mobile App · Cross-platform

Robert Schumann Haus — Museum Guide App

Overview

A cross-platform smartphone app for the Robert-Schumann-Haus Zwickau, designed to provide visitors with multimedia content, a virtual room-by-room tour, and an interactive map — in both German and English.

My Role

I led all design phases: UX research, user flows, lo-fi & hi-fi prototyping, and UI design. I also served as Scrum Master — responsible for sprint planning, backlog management, retrospectives, and Kanban. The development team handled testing, documentation, and Flutter/Dart implementation.

Research

I researched 10+ music museum apps worldwide, analysing their strengths and weaknesses to inform our design direction. This competitive analysis was foundational to every key decision in the project.

Process

Reference research → Low-fidelity prototypes → Usability testing with 14 participants across all age groups (teens to 80+) → Iteration → High-fidelity Figma prototype → Developer handoff.

Solution

A bilingual (DE/EN) app featuring: a virtual tour (Rundgang) navigating room by room, an interactive museum map (Karte), dark mode support, and multimedia exhibit content — designed to be intuitive even for first-time smartphone users.
